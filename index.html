Research

In computer graphics, the objects often possess intricate physical or geometric properties. When conventional numerical methods prove inadequate, learning-based methods can provide novel insights and alternative solutions. My research lies at the intersection of computer graphics and AI, with a focus on leveraging learning techniques to implicitly represent the mathematical and physical properties of objects. The approachs are applied to high-fidelity simulation, animation, geometry processing, and scientific discovery. Schedule time with me .

PC-NCLaws: Physics-Embedded Conditional Neural Constitutive Laws for Elastoplastic Materials
Xueguang Xie*, Shu Yan*, Shiwen Jia, Siyu Yang, Aimin Hao, Yang Gao, Peng Yu ***Pacific Graphics 2025***
TL;DR: We propose a generalizable framework called PC-NCLaws, which combines the partial differential equations with neural networks. PC-NCLaws enables inverse estimation of physical parameters from motion sequences, supporting objects with unknown or unmeasured properties.
